<p style="color:#F00;"><strong>旧约 - 以赛亚书(Isaiah) 第35章</strong></p>


<p>第1段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>旷野和乾旱之地，必然欢喜。沙漠也必快乐。又像玫瑰开花。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>The wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the rose.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第2段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>必开花繁盛，乐上加乐，而且欢呼。黎巴嫩的荣耀，并迦密与沙仑的华美，必赐给他。人必看见耶和华的荣耀，我们神的华美。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>It shall blossom abundantly, and rejoice even with joy and singing: the glory of Lebanon shall be given unto it, the excellency of Carmel and Sharon, they shall see the glory of the LORD, and the excellency of our God.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第3段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>你们要使软弱的手坚壮，无力的膝稳固。<a></a></td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>Strengthen ye the weak hands, and confirm the feeble knees.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第4段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>对胆怯的人说，你们要刚强，不要惧怕。看哪，你们的神必来报仇，必来施行极大的报应，他必来拯救你们。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>Say to them that are of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear not: behold, your God will come with vengeance, even God with a recompence; he will come and save you.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第5段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>那时瞎子的眼必睁开，聋子的耳必开通。<a></a></td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened, and the ears of the deaf shall be unstopped.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第6段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>那时瘸子必跳跃像鹿，哑吧的舌头必能歌唱。在旷野必有水发出，在沙漠必有河涌流。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>Then shall the lame man leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb sing: for in the wilderness shall waters break out, and streams in the desert.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第7段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>发光的沙，（或作蜃楼）要变为水池，乾渴之地，要变为泉源。在野狗躺卧之处，必有青草，芦苇，和蒲草。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>And the parched ground shall become a pool, and the thirsty land springs of water: in the habitation of dragons, where each lay, shall be grass with reeds and rushes.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第8段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>在那里必有一条大道，称为圣路。污秽人不得经过，必专为赎民行走，行路的人虽愚昧，也不至失迷。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>And an highway shall be there, and a way, and it shall be called The way of holiness; the unclean shall not pass over it; but it shall be for those: the wayfaring men, though fools, shall not err therein.</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第9段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">中文：</td>
		<td>在那里必没有狮子，猛兽也不登这路，在那里都遇不见。只有赎民在那里行走。</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>No lion shall be there, nor any ravenous beast shall go up thereon, it shall not be found there; but the redeemed shall walk there:</td>
	</tr>
</table>


<p>第10段</p>
<table border="1" width="98%" id="table1" style="border-collapse: collapse">

	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td width="73" align="center">英文：</td>
		<td>And the ransomed of the LORD shall return, and come to Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall flee away.</td>
	</tr>
</table>
